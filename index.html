Understanding and nurturing your online identity and relationships

Welcome to your guide on managing how you present yourself online and build healthy digital relationships. Our goal is to support you in creating a positive digital presence that reflects who you truly are.

Why Managing Your Online Identity & Relationships Matters

In today's digital age, your online identity shapes how others perceive you and influences your real-life relationships. Managing your digital footprint helps you present yourself authentically while protecting your privacy.

Research indicates that over 70% of young people have experienced some form of online misrepresentation or misunderstanding (Source: Digital Youth Study, 2022). Building genuine digital relationships requires honesty, respect, and awareness of your online presence.

Key Facts & Statistics

  • Approximately 60% of teens edit or delete posts to improve their online image (Pew Research, 2021).
  • Nearly 80% of teens believe that their online reputation impacts their future opportunities (Common Sense Media, 2022).

Ask the Digital Wellbeing Coach

Dear Wellbeing Coach,

I'm worried about how much time I spend on social media. I feel like it's affecting my mood and my sleep. How can I cut back without missing out on my friends' updates?

Hi! Try setting specific time limits for social media using your device’s built-in settings or apps. Turn off notifications during study or sleep hours to minimize distractions. Remember, quality interactions matter more than quantity—focus on meaningful connections rather than endless scrolling!

Dear Wellbeing Coach,

I often see people sharing personal details online that I think are too private. How can I protect my own privacy while still sharing enough to be social?

Great question! Be mindful of the information you share—avoid posting your exact location, contact details, or daily routines. Use privacy settings to control who sees your posts, and think twice before sharing anything you wouldn’t want a wider audience to see. Protecting your privacy helps you stay safe online!

Dear Wellbeing Coach,

Sometimes I feel pressured to respond immediately to messages or comments. How do I manage this without feeling overwhelmed?

It’s okay to take your time! Set boundaries by letting friends know when you're unavailable. Use status updates or autoresponders if needed. Remember, your mental health comes first—it's better to respond thoughtfully rather than quickly and stressfully.
